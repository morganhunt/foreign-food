Appetizer

Salami with Russian Bread and Butter

This appetizer is very simple to make. It's composed of salami, sliced and put on buttered Russian bread, preferably black. It is commonly eaten as a breakfast food, but can easily be adapted to an appetizer for a large meal.

Entre

Borscht

Borscht is a famous Russian dish, though it actually originated in Ukraine. It is a red, beet-based soup, with various other ingredients such as carrots, beef, and cabbage adding flavor and substance. It is served hot, with a scoop of sour cream on top. There are dozens of variations of the recipe of the soup itself, however. One of these many delicious variations can be found here.

Side Dish

Olivier Salad

This dish is a salad made with pickles, potatoes, eggs, carrots, cucumbers and bologna. The ingredients are then diced and mixed in a bowl with a fair amount of mayonnaise. Olivier salad is a common dish in a Russian meal. Exact ratios of ingredients vary widely, but here is one of many working recipes.

Dessert

Kiev Cake

This cake originated in Kiev, Ukraine in the twentieth century but has since become very popular across both Ukraine and Russia. The cake is composed in layers: light, airy cake layers, hazelnuts, jam, and a filling that resembles buttercream. Most people simply go and buy this cake from a store, but making it isn't much of a hassle, either.
